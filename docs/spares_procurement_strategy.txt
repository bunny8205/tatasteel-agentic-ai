Steel maintenance spares and procurement strategy.

Classify spares by criticality, lead time, consumption rate, failure consequence, and substitute availability.

Critical long-lead spares must be reserved before planned shutdown. Zero-stock critical spares require immediate purchase requisition and management visibility. High-cost spares should be linked to condition monitoring evidence and shutdown plans.

For each maintenance recommendation, state available stock, lead time, repairability, substitute rules, and whether procurement should be immediate, planned, or only monitored.

Procurement priority should increase when an asset is a bottleneck, RUL is low, safety risk is high, previous delay hours are high, or no substitute is approved.
